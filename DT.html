Design Thinking for Emory Goizueta



Description:

Design thinking uses the mindset and methods of design practitioners to identify and address human needs. At its core, design thinking encourages organizations to focus on human needs as a source of innovation to solve ambiguous problems.



I took the Business Design Thinking course at Emory my 3rd year wanting to learn about the approach to develop practical, creative solutions to real-world problems in an effective manner and hoped to learn skills to complement my technical-based Computer Science/Economic courses.



As a part of the coursework, we worked in teams for a final project which addressed one of Emory Universityâ€™s current strategic challenges. My team worked on improving our BBA/MPA program to be more inclusive of students not in the business school through the means of an improved website.



During the semester we interviewed students via Zoom, developed personas, came up with prototypes, tested all elements of our prototype, and finished with a final presentation. I often try to implement design thinking in the organizations I am a part of, and have seen amazing results! This is definitely one of my favorite courses at Emory.
