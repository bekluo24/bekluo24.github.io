CIMM Research Project

Summer 2019 || Baton Rouge, LA



Description:

As part of the NSF funded CIMM REU summer research experience, and in collaboration with the Department of Computer Science at Southern University and A&M College, we designed one refractory high entropy alloy (RHEA) ReMoNbTaV with three refractory elements (Nb, Ta, V).



The phase diagram shows that the RHEA maintains body-centered cubic when the temperature is above 1000 ℃ and has melting point at about 2600 ℃. The structural and mechanical properties of the BCC RHEA were performed by SSOS method and compared to the 100-atom supercell, the results conformed the same accuracy of the SSOS method and revealed the high strength, hardness, and ductileness of ReMoNbTaV.



I operated data transfers and calculation programs using the Louisiana Optical Network Infrastructure (LONI) and the Nano supercomputer and as a part of the REU program, participated in community outreach activities and was invited to speak to middle school students interested in engineering/computer science to encourage underrepresented minorities’ participation in the STEM field.
